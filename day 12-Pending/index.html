Kazi Nazrul Islam

The national poet of Bangladesh

Rebel Poet

Born in undivided Bengal in 1899, Kazi Nazrul Islam was fondly called the 'rebel poet' during India's freedom struggle. All his life, Islam was engaged in literary contributions on the themes of freedom, humanism, and social justice. His poetry focussed on the spirit of the Indian independence movement and the struggles of the common people. He composed many notable patriotic songs that inspired and mobilised people during the fight for independence.

Awesome Writer

His works gained recognition in the Soviet Union, where his poems were translated into Russian. He was awarded the "Gold Medal" by the Soviet Land Nehru Award in 1959 for his outstanding contribution to world literature. In 1972, posthumously, he was awarded honorary citizenship by the Turkish government for his support of the Turkish War of Independence through his writings.
